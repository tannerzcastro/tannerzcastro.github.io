About Me

Born and raised in California, I spent my early years playing a variety of sports until I found my true love, water polo! In school, I loved art and history, but excelled at math and science. Some of my favorite vacation spots as a kid included Lake Havasu in Arizona, and Ft. Lauderdale in Florida.

I started off as a computer science major at El Camino College. The plan was to become a video game programmer, but I didn't quite feel driven and excited by what I was doing. The next year I became an art major, and continued to gain credits until I transfered to Westwood College. There, I finished my bachelor's in game art and have since expanded into information technology via self-study.

As a young adult, I entered the work force as a lifeguard at a city pool. I guarded, taught swim lessons, and even managed the pool for 7 years. After that, my first full-time job was in electromagnetic assembly. Towards the end of my assembly work, COVID-19 hit, and I bounced between jobs until I found work in a small IT company.
